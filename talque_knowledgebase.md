## 1. Account & Settings

### 1.1 Delete your talque user account

The **Delete user account** function can only be performed by the user, and is **irreversible** — all data is permanently deleted.

**Step by step:**

1. Click on **Settings → Account**.
2. Scroll to the bottom of the page.
3. Click the **[Permanently delete account]** button.
4. You will receive a verification email asking you to confirm the deletion one more time.

> **Note:** If you have downloaded the mobile app, account data may still be cached there. To make the deletion fully effective, sign out of the app and uninstall it as well.

### 1.2 Change your email address in the talque app

If you want to keep your talque account but change your email address:

1. **Sign in** to your existing talque account.
2. **Add a secondary email:** Click your profile picture and then **Settings → Account**. Add your new email address as a secondary email.
3. **Confirm the activation email:** talque will send an activation email to your new address. Open it and click the activation link.
4. **Make the secondary address primary:** Once the secondary address is activated, you'll see a **[Set as Primary]** option next to it. Click it.
5. **Optional:** Delete the old email address.

### 1.3 Set up a password

talque recommends using a **Magic Link** for easy access. If you'd rather set a password, the process is quick and straightforward. You can reset your password at any time and view your login history in the **Security** tab.

**How to reach the password setup page:**

1. Sign in to talque by entering your email address. You'll receive a Magic Link in your inbox.
2. After signing in, click your profile picture.
3. Choose **Settings** from the dropdown menu.
4. Go to the **Security** tab.
5. There you'll find the option "Set password" and a button to request a password-reset link.

**Then:**

1. Open your email inbox and look for the talque email.
2. Click the link in the email to open the password setup page.
3. Enter your new password.

**Recommendations for a strong password:**

- **Mix of characters:** Combine uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters.
- **Long enough:** Use at least **12 characters**.
- **No simple patterns:** Avoid personal information or common words.
- **Passphrases:** Combine random words or use a short sentence.

### 1.4 Change the system language

Click your profile picture, then **Settings → Language**.

### 1.5 Local time zone

talque displays times in both the **event time zone** and your **local time zone**.

- For **1:1 calls**, the proposed time slots appear in your local time zone (matched to your conversation partner's availability in their time zone).
- Your local time zone is also taken into account during the availability questions in onboarding.

### 1.6 Manage email notifications

If you haven't turned off the notification function, you'll receive an email whenever, for example, a chat message comes in.

**Turn off email notifications:** Profile picture → **Settings → Notifications**.

> **Tip:** Instead of turning off email notifications completely, you can filter them automatically into a separate inbox folder. That way, you still receive event notifications with iCal attachments, and your appointments appear in your Google or MS calendar.

### 1.7 Keep your account but leave an event

You can leave an event by clicking your profile picture and then **Settings → Networks → [Leave Network]**. Your talque account, including chat history and contacts, remains intact — you just won't be visible in that event anymore.

### 1.8 Access after the event

How long the event remains online depends on the event organizer. After the event ends, you can generally continue using talque and have access to:

- Chat messages you have exchanged
- Contacts you made during the event

## 2. Getting Started with talque

### 2.1 Structure of the talque web app

The talque web app consists of **three main areas**:

| Area | Position | Function |
|---|---|---|
| **Event main menu** | Center | Shows all functions relevant to the event: agenda, participant list, news wall, partner pages |
| **Chat area** | Top right (next to profile picture) | Independent of the current event — stay in touch with contacts even after the event. Also contains your personal calendar and account settings |
| **Event selector** | Top left | Switch between different events once you've attended multiple talque events |

### 2.2 Set up a talque event in 5 steps

1. **Get started:** Basic event setup
2. **Basic content structure:** Foundational content
3. **Involving content contributors:** Bring in speakers and partners
4. **Design your event's features:** Configure features
5. **Inviting participants:** Invite attendees

> The order of tabs in the admin area follows this process. Start with the **General** tab, then **Branding**, and so on.

### 2.3 Timeline for event preparation

- **Right after kick-off:** Build the foundation (speaker profiles, sessions, partner profiles with basic data).
- **Then:** Bring in speakers and partner editors so they can complete their profiles.
- **1–2 weeks before event start:** Invite attendees — via the **Invitations** tab in the admin area.

> **Important:** Speakers and partner editors work via a link-based flow — they do **not** need to register on talque, they can start editing right away. Only attendees need to register, once you open the doors to the event.

### 2.4 Successful event participation

**Tips for exhibitors and partners:**

- **Engaging videos:** Show videos with real value — not standard promotional clips.
- **Exclusive information:** Offer content that isn't available elsewhere.
- **Active participation:** Run workshops, discussion rounds, or presentations on interesting aspects of your company. Attendees of these sessions are likely interested and represent promising leads.
- **Check the booking list:** Available directly in talque. Subject to terms and exhibitor package, the organizer may also provide a Session View List after the event.
- **Personalized messages:** Reach out directly via **Best Matches** to attendees with potential interest.

**Staff preparation:**

- Fill out the matchmaking questions as accurately as possible.
- Test Jitsi calls in advance (see Chapter 11).

## 3. Networking & Meetings

### 3.1 Connect with other attendees

In the **Participants tab**, click on a participant and then choose how to connect, depending on the event type:

- **[Chat]** — direct chat message
- **[Request Meeting]** — schedule an appointment
- **[Request Video Call]** — start a video call

The available options depend on whether the event is **on-site, hybrid, or virtual**.

Through **partner and sponsor profiles**, you can reach the partner staff via:

- **Direct call function** (if enabled)
- **Chat** or **1:1 meeting request**

### 3.2 Schedule 1:1 meetings

1. Open the desired profile (attendee or partner staff member).
2. Click **[Request Meeting]**.
3. Pick one of the available time slots.
4. The request is sent to the other person, who can accept or decline.

### 3.3 Set up meeting times (admin)

**Path:** `Admin → Participant Meetings → Settings → On-premise networking times`

Add the meeting times there. They apply to all meetings scheduled via the **[Request Meeting]** button.

> **Tips:**
> - On-site meeting times should fall within the physical event only.
> - Online meeting times can also include the day before and a few days after the event.
> - The **[Request Meeting]** button on partner and attendee profiles only appears once meeting times have been scheduled — and they must be in the future.

### 3.4 Set up meeting spots (admin)

Meeting spots are locations for 1:1 conversations. They can be:

- **Areas** like a coffee corner or business lounge with a set number of seats (not numbered)
- **Low-priority spots** as simple orientation points (e.g., a "Meeting Spot" sign by a door)
- **Numbered tables**, e.g., in a networking area, a lounge for exclusive partners, or tables at an exhibitor booth

**Path:** `Admin → Meeting Spots`

**Fields per meeting spot:**

| Field | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Priority** | Order in which spots are booked based on availability. **Higher number = booked first.** |
| **Limit** | Number of parallel meetings in the room |
| **Capacity** | Number of people who fit in the room/at the table (typically 2× the limit) |

> **Note:** Limit and capacity can be left blank, e.g., for general outdoor spots.

**Specific meeting spot times:**

If a spot isn't continuously available, click the **[+]** button at the bottom right and add one or more meeting times.

**Reserving meeting spots for partner companies:**

Click the **[+]** button and add times. One or more partner companies can be assigned per time slot.

### 3.5 Mark favorites

You can mark attendees as favorites by clicking the **star on the profile picture** in the participant detail view.

**View favorites:** Use the **star toggle in the top right** of the participants page.

> **Downloading** the favorites list is not possible.

### 3.6 Calendar integration

The integration is designed to **import your appointments** so that the best 1:1 meeting suggestions can be made based on them.

- Your own appointments are visible to no one but you.
- To export 1:1 meetings and talque session bookings to your **Outlook/MS calendar**, use the email notification with the **iCal attachment**.

### 3.7 What does the talque calendar show?

The calendar (second icon in the top right) shows:

- **1:1 meetings**
- **Booked sessions**

Not shown:

- Past appointments
- Sessions you're on the waitlist for

### 3.8 Direct calls

Direct calls are an **optional feature** often included in premium exhibitor packages. They let attendees call a company's contact persons directly, without scheduling an appointment.

**List staff as available:**

When a staff member is listed as a contact person on a partner profile, they're automatically listed under **Direct Calls** as soon as they activate the **Available for profile video calls** toggle.

**Toggle availability on/off:**

Profile picture in the top right → flip the **Available for profile video calls** switch. Useful for breaks too.

### 3.9 Exchange files and documents

To the left of the text input in the chat, you'll find a blue **[+]** button. With it you can:

- Send files
- Start video calls directly

Works once a **two-way chat exchange** has happened with the other person.

### 3.10 VIP Networking Tables

VIP Networking Tables let senior attendees meet several other attendees in 1:1 meetings within a compressed time frame.

**Typical use cases:**

- Business angel or CEO meets several attendees in 1:1 meetings over an afternoon
- Recruiting: job interviews with HR staff at an exhibitor booth

**Setup components:**

- **Partner profile** that serves as a booking page on the VIP Networking Tables tab
- **VIP as partner staff** on this profile
- **Virtual meeting spots**

#### Partner profile for the VIP Networking Table

1. Create a new partner profile or use an existing one.
2. Enter the **screening question**: `Networking Table → Question when applying for networking table`.
3. In the **Contact Details** section, uncheck:
   - "Participants can select whether they are contact persons during signup"
   - "Contact profiles have a Chat button as well"
   - "Contact profiles have a Meet button as well"

#### Create a meeting spot

1. **Path:** `Admin → Meeting Spot → [+ Add]`
2. **Name:** e.g., "Investors Lounge — Table 1"
3. **Priority:** 1
4. **Capacity:** 2
5. **Limit:** 1
6. Click **[+]** at the bottom right to add a reserved time.
7. In the **Partners** field, choose the matching partner profile.
8. Set opening hours and slot duration.
9. **Check the box:** "Dedicated networking table (can only be applied for on partner)" — otherwise the reserved time won't appear on the VIP tab.

#### Assign the interviewer (VIP)

Once the VIP has registered on talque, they can be added as partner staff in the **Contact Profiles** field on the partner profile. The **[Request Meeting]** button only appears after staff have been added.

> Want to rename the tab (e.g., "VIP Investor Tables", "HR Zone")? Share the desired label with your talque event manager.

### 3.11 Are my matchmaking choices visible?

In onboarding, you set up your individual participant profile and define your matchmaking criteria. Your profile is **visible to other users on the platform for the duration of the digital event**, so they can connect with you.

### 3.12 How does matchmaking work?

In the **Best Matches** tab on the participants page, you'll see other attendees with potential shared interests.

- It's based on an **algorithm** built into the platform.
- It uses content and information that attendees provide during **onboarding**.
- Personalized matches are generated on this basis.

**Run matchmaking again:** Profile picture → **Edit profile**.

## 4. Sessions & Program

### 4.1 Join a session

1. Go to the **Agenda** menu item.
2. Pick the session relevant to you.
3. The **[Enter Session]** button appears **3 minutes before** the session starts.

> Speakers and moderators see the button **30 minutes before**.

If you booked the session in advance (clicking **[Attend]**), you'll get a notification when it starts.

### 4.2 Limited attendance

Some interactive workshops have limited capacity. Please register in advance via **[Attend]** to reserve your spot.

The actual **capacity** and number of **available spots** are shown in the "Attendees" area of the session detail view.

### 4.3 See booked sessions

**Two ways:**

1. Flip the **favorites toggle** on the Agenda menu
2. Open your **personal calendar** (icon in the top right) — shows both sessions and requested 1:1 meetings

### 4.4 Set up a small event manually

If you're planning a simple event with a handful of sessions:

1. Go to **Admin → Schedule** and click **[+ Add]**.
2. Enter at least the session **title**, ideally with date and time.
3. To link a session to speakers or moderators, create a speaker profile first.
4. In the session profile: **Basic Details → Speakers** — start typing the name and pick the speaker from the list.

You can then bring in speakers via the **Speaker Form email**.

### 4.5 Set up a larger event

For larger events we recommend:

1. **Plan structure first:** Define rooms, formats, and additional session filters.
2. **Create filters and rooms** before you create sessions.
3. **Upload sessions via file** (Excel template).

> **Important:**
> - Only upload data once everything is complete.
> - Test the upload feature beforehand with a small batch of speakers and sessions.
> - You **cannot update existing session data with a new file** — only add new sessions.
> - Speakers and sessions can only be deleted **one at a time**.

**Upload path:** `Admin → Schedule → [Upload CSV]`

### 4.6 Rooms, formats, and program filters

#### Rooms

For on-site events: physical rooms like "Plenary" or "Hall Amsterdam".

For virtual events: virtual rooms used for structuring (e.g., "Plenary", "Track 1", "Track 2").

Rooms can be used to limit the number of attendees in a session.

**Path:** `Admin → Rooms and Formats → Rooms`

Then list them in the **Rooms** column of the session Excel file.

#### Formats

Formats structure the agenda and color-code it.

| Presentation formats | Interactive formats | Networking formats |
|---|---|---|
| Keynote | Workshop | Networking Break |
| Panel | Roundtable | Speed Dating |
| Panel Discussion | Roundtable Discussion | Get Together |
| CEO Interview | Forum | Open Space |
| Lecture | Breakout Session | Unconferencing |
| Presentation | Think Tank | |
| Masterclass | Deep Dive | |

**Path:** `Admin → Rooms and Formats → Formats`

Then list them in the **Formats** column of the session Excel file.

#### Topics

Topics let attendees browse by specific themes. They can also be assigned by speakers.

- Topic tags **don't** show on the program overview page — only in the **session detail view**.

**Path:** `Admin → Classification Tags → Sessions → Speaker Assigned Classification Tags`

#### Program filter buttons

Based on filter tags, they let you quickly select parts of the event.

**Path:** `Admin → General → Classification Tags → Official Classification Tags`

Program filter buttons are configured by your event manager based on these tags.

### 4.7 Session access via tickets

There are **two ways** to control session access:

1. **By ticket category:** Attendees hold a ticket; each session can be assigned to specific tickets. Attendees only see sessions they're entitled to.
2. **Locked sessions:** Attendees can't sign themselves up — the organizer adds them to the sessions (manually, via invitation upload, or via API).

**Use cases:**

- Trade fair and conference run in parallel: trade-fair-only ticket holders shouldn't see the conference program.
- Pitch competition: each contestant only sees their own pitching session.
- Dinner party: everyone sees it on the program, but only those who booked can attend.

#### Working with ticket categories

1. **Create ticket categories:** `Admin → Classification Tags → Participants → Ticket Categories → [+ Add]`. Examples: "Conference", "Trade Fair".
2. **Configure sessions:** By default, sessions are open to everyone. To restrict them: `Admin → Agenda → open session → Additional Options → Only for Tickets …`. Pick the eligible ticket categories.
3. **Assign tickets:**
   - Manually in invitations: `Admin → Invitations → open invitation → Ticket`
   - Via upload file: in the invitation CSV/XLSX
   - Via API from an external ticketing system

> **Caution:** If you change a participant's ticket during the event (e.g., an upgrade), you have to update it in **both the participant profile and the invitation**.

> Attendees without a ticket category can see **all** sessions.

#### Configure restricted sessions

To set up a session that participants cannot self-register for:

1. Go to **Admin → Agenda** and open the session.
2. Under **Additional Options → Participation**, choose: **"Assigned by admin, but visible to all for participation"**.
3. If the session includes a video meeting: in **Video Editor → Video call in session**, choose **"Video call room (registration required)"**.

> Restricted sessions don't show the **[+]** button — attendees can't sign up themselves.
>
> **Caution:** With embedded livestreams, access can't be controlled this way — anyone who sees the session can watch the stream.

#### Manually assign attendees to a session

**Before registration:**

1. **Path:** `Admin → Invitations` → open the invitation profile
2. In the **Sessions** field, type the session name and pick it.

> Currently it's **not possible** to assign sessions in the uploaded invitation .csv file.

**After registration:**

1. Open **Agenda in the main menu** (user view).
2. Pick the relevant session.
3. In the **Attendees** tab, click **[+]** and add participants.

> **Tip:** If you can't find a participant, first open the participant list and scroll all the way down so all attendees load. Then go back to the session.

### 4.8 Set up a pitch contest

In a talque pitch contest, the pitch participants are presented on a partner tab. During the event, each attendee receives a number of pitch points to give to contestants.

**Configuration options:**

- How many pitch points per attendee (recommended: **10 points**)
- How many points can be given to a single contestant max
- Name of the pitch points button (e.g., "Pitch Points")
- Name of the partner category

**Setup:**

1. Pick the partner tab (default: Exhibitors or Sponsors) you want to use, and rename it (e.g., "Pitch", "Startups"). **Path:** `Admin → Exhibitors & Sponsors → Settings`.
2. Set up the base profiles and bring in the pitch contestants to contribute content — same workflow as for partners (including images and videos).
3. Share the configuration with talque.

> **Important:** Don't give out **more than 10 pitch points per person**. Otherwise an unintended imbalance can result if someone gives all points to a single contestant.

### 4.9 Moderators

Moderators are set up exactly like speakers: add them via the speaker list, then in the session profile under `Basic Details → Moderators` (or in the corresponding CSV).

### 4.10 Partner sessions

Sessions can be linked to partner profiles:

1. Open the session profile.
2. **Path:** `Basic Details → Associated Partner` → pick the partner from the dropdown.

When a session is linked to a partner profile, the overview shows **the partner logo** instead of the speaker image.

### 4.11 Text formatting with Markdown

Markdown syntax in talque text fields enables simple formatting.

#### Emphasis

```markdown
*This text will be bold*
_This will be italic_
+This text will be underlined+
```

#### Lists

**Unordered:**

```markdown
* Item 1
* Item 2
* Item 3
```

**Ordered:**

```markdown
1. Item 1
2. Item 2
3. Item 3
```

#### Block quotes

Block quotes start with one or three greater-than signs:

```markdown
Albert Einstein:
>>> I would rather teach peace than war.
>>> I would rather instill love than hate.
```

#### Links

```markdown
[talque](https://www.talque.com)
```

#### Backslash as an escape character

Markdown allows backslash escapes to display characters that would otherwise have special meaning:

```markdown
\+\+\+ breaking news \+\+\+
```

**Escapable characters:** `\` `*` `+` `-` `` ` `` `_` `#` `{}` `[]` `()`

### 4.12 Hyperlinks in talque text fields

Format: `[link text](URL)` — example: `[talque](https://www.talque.com)`

Make sure formatting is enabled in the text field.

**Where you can use hyperlinks:**

- Speaker bio (speaker profile)
- Partner profiles (exhibitors, sponsors): short description — also in the partner form
- Agenda session: summary

### 4.13 Approve edited content

In the session overview, you'll spot edited sessions that need attention by a **yellow marker 🟡**.

When editors have added or changed content:

1. Open the session detail view.
2. The **published version** and the **version edited by speaker/partner** appear side by side on two tabs.
3. Edit the new version as you wish.
4. Click **[Approve]** to accept the changes — or **[Reject]** to dismiss them.

> **Tip:** The human eye is great at spotting visual differences. Zoom out a bit in your browser and toggle between the official and edited versions to spot changes.

> Changes to speaker profiles do **not** need to be approved — only session changes.

### 4.14 Upload a session handout

You can upload a handout for your session, which attendees can download.

**How to do it:**

1. You'll receive a link to the **session form** by email from the organizer (same link as for editing your speaker profile).
2. The form has a **Technical Requirements** area where you can upload the file.

> **Important:** Only the **public handout** is visible to attendees.

> Depending on the organizer's setting, the handout is visible immediately or only when the session starts.

## 5. Partners & Exhibitors

### 5.1 Basics — what do partner profiles offer?

Partner profiles on talque let companies showcase the following:

- **Company name**, logo, teaser image, and short description
- **Media:** videos, files, photos
- **Subcategories** like products or job postings
- **Linked program items** and booth agenda

**Attendee interaction options:**

- **Send meeting requests** (admin can confirm and assign)
- **View staff** and contact them via chat
- **Use an automatic company chat**

**Premium features:** live statistics on profile visitors for targeted outreach.

### 5.2 Partner categories

There are two partner profile categories: **Exhibitors** and **Sponsors**.

Within each, typical tiers exist for differentiating by engagement, contribution, or type:

- Basic
- Silver
- Gold
- Platinum

> If you don't need categories, create at least **one** category with the same name as the menu tab.

### 5.3 Set up a partner profile

All partner-related actions happen under **Admin → Sponsors and Exhibitors**.

**Manual creation:** click **[+ Add]**.

**Required fields:**

- Company name
- Sponsor or exhibitor category
- Partner editor contact

**Recommended additional fields:**

- General company email (info@…)
- Company website
- Address

> **Tip:** If you can't find a partner profile in the frontend, check that it's **published** and has a **partner category** assigned.

### 5.4 Three types of contact information

Partner profiles include three contact types:

1. **General company contact details** (info@ address, website, address)
2. **Company staff during the event** (e.g., booth staff)
3. **Editor contact** (person who edits the profile — usually marketing)

#### Adding a partner editor contact

In the **Partner Editor Contact** field, you can add one or more people responsible for the profile content. Enter the email address and confirm with **ENTER**.

### 5.5 Settings tab — partner categories

Here you configure:

- The name of the partner tab
- The partner categories

> **Tip:** Even if a category isn't strictly necessary, create one and name it to match the tab.

### 5.6 Upload partner data

**Path:** `Admin → Sponsors and Exhibitors → [⋮] → [Upload CSV]`

Accepted formats: **.xlsx** and **.csv**.

> Use our templates.

**Required fields:** `company name` and `editor contact`.

> **Tips:**
> - Don't change or delete the **header row**.
> - Add as many details as possible.

### 5.7 Publish a partner profile

To make the profile visible to attendees, click **[Publish]** at the top right of the partner profile.

### 5.8 Column formats for partner upload

| Column | Content | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 3 & 4 | Sponsor and exhibitor categories | One can be left blank. Categories must be created in advance under `Admin → Sponsors & Exhibitors → Settings`. |
| 5 | Language | IETF tag (`EN_US`, `DE_DE`, `ES_ES`, `FR_FR`, `PT_PT`) — only event languages allowed. Can be left blank. |
| 7 | Company contact email | Shown publicly on the partner profile. |
| 11 | Country | ISO 3166-1 alpha-2 (e.g., `DE`, `US`, `GB`, `ES`, `FR`). Can be left blank. |
| 13–18 | Partner editor name & email | Contact details of the person who maintains the profile (usually marketing). |

### 5.9 Send invitations to partner admins (profile editors)

#### What partner admins can do

Partner admins are invited to manage their partner profile and can:

- **Profile management:** update company information, customize the profile
- **Content upload:** presentations, brochures, other key documents
- **Team management:** add new team members
- **Event interactions:** connect with attendees and other partners

#### Email setup prep

**Email templates:**

- **Path:** `Admin → Email Templates` → **Partner Contact Information** tab
- Write and save the **invitation text for the profile admins** here.

**Email banner:**

- **Path:** `Admin → Branding`
- Scroll to **Email Branding** and upload your image to customize the email design.

#### Send emails

**For all partners at once:**

`Admin → Sponsors & Exhibitors` → at the bottom: **[Send Emails] → [Send New Partner Emails]**

**For a single partner:**

Open the partner profile → at the bottom: **Send Email → Send Partner Email**

> This also lets you **resend** an invitation if needed.

#### Check invitation status

The **invitation list** shows the status:

- **"Sent"** → invitation sent
- Resend option available

The profile admin receives an email with a link to register and edit the partner profile.

#### Access for partner admins

Depending on configuration, the partner admin sees one of these:

- **Without access times:** Routed to the network or the home page; sees a button for the partner profile. With multiple profiles, multiple buttons.
- **With access times:** Routed straight to the partner profile and can make changes via the **pencil icon** and **drag and drop**.

### 5.10 Add company admins and staff

In the right area of the partner profile under the **Details** title, you can:

- Set up the company description
- Add additional **profile editors (company admins)** and **staff**

#### Company admins

- When you add a new admin who hasn't joined the event yet, they're automatically sent an invitation.
- The invitation email is **personal** and must not be forwarded.
- **Partner admins are not visible to attendees.**

**Status indicators:**

- "not joined yet" → admin hasn't joined the network yet
- Once they join, their profile is added to the partner.

> **Important on partner meetings:** Visitor meeting requests can only be answered by company admins and assigned to staff. Changes can only be made by **registered** company admins.

#### Staff (booth staff)

Staff can be added directly in the profile. There are **four ways** to attach booth staff to partner profiles:

1. **Manually during the event:** Add them in the **Contact Profiles** field on the partner profile. Requires that the person is already registered for the event.
2. **Manually before the event:** Open the person's invitation (`Admin → Invitations`) and add the partner company in the **Contact Person of** field.
3. **Self-attribution** (recommended for small to medium events): Check **"Participants can select whether they are contact persons during signup"** on the partner profile. Attendees can then attach themselves to a partner profile during onboarding.
4. **Via partner tickets** (recommended for large trade fairs): Requires specific configuration of your ticket shop. Partner admins order tickets for their staff. Via API to talque, the staff are automatically attached to the partner profile.

> **Important:** When staff are added directly in a partner profile but haven't joined the network yet, they do **not** automatically get an invitation to the network. Once they join the network on their own, they'll appear as staff.

> The "not joined yet" status (in red) is only visible to admins — not to attendees.

### 5.11 Delete vs. remove participants

#### Delete a participant profile

**Full deletion** of a profile can **only be done by the user themselves**.

If the user is a member of multiple networks and has accumulated contacts/chat messages there, the deletion affects all that data.

#### Remove from a specific network

**Option 1 (by admin):**

1. Open the profile in the participant list.
2. **Path:** `Admin → Delete`

### 7.6 Stream to YouTube

> Before streaming: your YouTube account must be enabled.

#### Activate streaming

1. **Sign in to YouTube.**
2. Profile picture → **YouTube Studio**.
3. **[Create]** → **Go Live**.
4. If you've never streamed before: message "Livestreaming is not yet available" → **[Activate]**.
5. **Enter your mobile number** to validate.
6. **Wait 24 hours**, then return to the page.

#### Stream via RTMP (5 steps)

1. **Sign in to YouTube.**
2. Camera icon, top right.
3. **Go Live**.
4. **[Edit]:**
   - Set the stream name.
   - Visibility: **Unlisted** (only people with the link can see it).
   - Enable **Allow Embedding**.
   - Under **Customization**: enable/disable live chat as needed.
   - Optional: upload a thumbnail.
5. Copy the **Stream Key and RTMP URL** into your streaming or meeting software. The stream starts automatically when a signal is sent.

1. **Sign in to YouTube.**
2. Camera icon, top right.

4. Camera icon, top left.
5. **Configuration:**
   - Pick a title, upload a thumbnail.

   - Toggle live chat on/off as needed.
   - Visibility: **Unlisted**.
   - Schedule the livestream time.
   - **[Done]**.
6. Check the **preview**, verify devices and time, then click **[Go Live]**.

1. Open the watch link of the stream.
2. **Share → Embed**.
3. Copy the link between `src="…"` and paste it into the talque session.

Available in **YouTube Studio** or directly below the livestream window on the watch page.

### 7.7 Embed a livestream in talque

Livestreams are the right choice for:

- Large audiences
- Seamless integration in talque
- Parallel use on social media

For livestreaming, you send your encoded video (via **RTMP**) to a streaming platform like Vimeo or YouTube Live. The platform transcodes it into multiple formats and resolutions.

**Livestream credentials:**

- **RTMPS URL** and **stream key** for transmission
- **Embed link** for embedding in talque

**Path:** `Admin → Schedule → Session` → Video → **Session Video** → paste the embed link.

> **Recommendation:** Since livestreams often run across several consecutive sessions (e.g., a full afternoon), use the **"Video can be watched after session start"** option. Optional: add a preview thumbnail and choose **"Video can be watched at any time"** for the first session of the day.

> If you've booked a livestream from us, we deliver the embed link with the RTMP credentials.

### 7.8 Livestream production: options

There are several ways to produce livestreams — with different technical effort:

- **Professional hardware encoders**
- **Studio software** (used by video software providers)
- **Self-produced** via a Zoom video call with speakers

#### Streaming from Zoom

One of the simplest methods:

1. Invite speakers and moderators into a Zoom call.
2. Stream the call directly from the **Zoom cloud** to the streaming platform (e.g., Vimeo).

If you've booked livestreams from us, we set things up and provide the credentials for the Zoom web interface.

**Pros:**

- No high-speed internet connection needed
- No video production team needed
- No complex new software

#### Streaming from studio software

Studio software like **OBS, Vimeo Studio, or YouTube Creator Studio** lets you produce high-quality, professional-looking livestreams.

- Switch between different input sources
- Encodes the output to RTMP
- **Input sources:** live camera feeds, video calls, pre-recorded videos, break slides

> **Important:** With simultaneous inbound and outbound video calls, make sure your internet bandwidth is sufficient.

> **Recommendation:** Studio software is complex — bring in a professional video production team.

### 7.9 Session chat vs. external tools (Slido, Mentimeter)

#### talque session chat

- Direct interaction between attendees and moderators during a session
- **Automatically enabled in every session**, can be turned off individually in admin
- Three placeholder options that prompt attendees to ask questions
- Ideal for simple **Q&A sessions**

#### External polling tools

For polls and other interactive features, you can integrate external tools. Common ones:

- **Slido**
- **Mentimeter**

Just paste the poll URL into the sessions where it should appear.

#### Notes on usage

- The talque session chat is **not cross-session** — messages only appear in the current session.
- If an external tool is used with the same URL across **multiple sessions**, questions from all sessions are visible in the tool.
- For multilingual networks, the chat is **cross-language**.
- If session chat **and** an external link are both active, the external link takes **priority**.

#### Disable chat

- **talque session chat:** uncheck the box.
- **External tool:** delete the link.

> The chat window only becomes visible **when the session starts** and stays active until disabled.

### 7.10 Integrate Slido (Q&A and polls)

For livestream broadcasts, **Slido** enables audience interaction via Q&A, polls, and discussions.

**Prerequisite:** a Slido account (free or paid) and a Slido event.

**Capabilities:**

- Integrate the participant mode of your Slido event into talque sessions — it shows in the talque sidebar.
- Embed the current mode into your video conference/livestream to display poll results.
- A Slido event can include a Q&A session and several polls — you control which one is active.
- Slido Q&A sessions can be **moderated**.

> The integration is an **optional feature** in talque.

#### Integrate a Slido URL in talque

1. In your Slido event: **Share → Share link with participants** → copy the embed link.
2. In talque: `Admin → Agenda` → open the session → **Additional Options → Poll URL** → paste.

#### Test Slido

The Slido UI only appears in the talque sidebar **once the session starts**. Create a test session at the current time to check the integration.

#### Advanced Slido options

**Set the default view:**

| URL extension | Effect |
|---|---|
| `/live/questions` | Direct access to Q&A |
| `/live/polls` | Direct access to polls |

**Direct access to specific Slido rooms** (Pro license or higher):

Append `?section=ROOMID` to the event link.

**How to find the room ID:**

1. In the Slido backend, pick the room.
2. Hover over **Presentation Mode**.
3. Choose **Present in a new tab**.
4. From the address bar, copy everything starting at `?section=`.

**Example:**

```
https://app.sli.do/event/eventname
https://app.sli.do/event/eventname?section=1c81ae52-5718-4897-8845-621e773e9d46
```

### 8.1 Invite attendees

In talque, there are two ways to bring people in:

1. **Right after event setup:** invite content contributors (speakers, partners) — link-based, **no talque registration required**.
2. **About a week before event start:** invite attendees into the talque app — via the **Invitations** tab in the admin area.

> **Tip:** The link in the registration email can also be used later to log in.

#### Email send prep

- **Email banner:** `Admin → Branding → Email Banner`.

> **Tip:** Click **[Show Preview]** at the bottom of the template to preview the email. You can also send a sample to yourself.

#### Add attendees

Two ways:

- Manually via **[+ Add]**
- Via file upload through **[Upload CSV]** (accepts: .xlsx, .csv)

**Required field:** `email` — but the more details, the better.

> **Tip:** Don't change or delete the header row — just add data in the rows below it.

#### Send invitations

`Admin → Invitations` → at the bottom **[Send Emails]**.

**Status changes:**

- `Not Sent` → `Sent` → `Registered`

> **Tip:** If status shows `Error`, check the email templates. Click **[Show Preview]** in the template — if an error appears, fix it. Common cause: a broken auto-text field.

### 8.2 Invite speakers and partner staff

Speakers and partner staff also need to be invited. There are several ways:

- By adding the partner company to the invitations
- Optional: invite the partner editor to the platform so they can review their profile from a user perspective

> **Recommendation:** Invite speakers, partner editors, and partner staff **a few days before other attendees** so they can review their profiles and get familiar with the platform.

Speakers on the speaker list automatically get the **Speaker Invitation** email template.

### 8.3 Set up onboarding

After registration, all attendees go through a few questions — we call this **onboarding**.

Onboarding starts with two **mandatory introductory questions** in which attendees confirm their name and job credentials.

#### Question types in onboarding

| Question area | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **Matchmaking** | Connects attendees with like-minded people — answers appear in the **Best Matches** tab and on the profile |
| **Availability** | Which days and times the attendee is available for meetings (on-site and/or via video call) |
| **Participation status** | Whether the attendee will be on-site |
| **Onboarding partner staff** | Attendees can attach themselves to a partner company |

### 8.4 Matchmaking questions

Matchmaking often starts with a **category question** to classify attendees (e.g., attendee, speaker, exhibitor, press, startup).

**Two basic types:**

| Type | How it works |
|---|---|
| **Keyword questions** | Attendees with the same answers are matched |
| **Seeking/Skill questions** | Attendees who offer a skill are matched with others looking for that skill |

You can also ask questions that **don't influence matchmaking** — those answers only appear on the profile.

> Onboarding often closes with an **open question** like "Tell us a bit about yourself…"

#### Category question

A typical category question covers attendees, speakers, and partners. You can ask different follow-up questions per category.

#### Similarity questions

Ask about **shared interests**. Attendees with the same answers are matched. A question about hobbies is a nice way to wrap up onboarding.

#### Seeking/Skills questions

Connects attendees who **offer a skill or asset** with attendees who could benefit. Match by skill, funding sought/offered, region, etc.

**Example:** Ask startups about their funding needs and business angels about the funding they offer — and connect the two.

#### [Other] option

For similarity and seeking/skills questions, you can add an **[Other]** option, where attendees can write in their own answer.

### 8.5 How are answers displayed?

The **tags** an attendee picks are used for matchmaking:

- In the **Best Matches** tab, attendees see other attendees with strong overlaps.
- A **matching score** and the **matching tags** are shown for each match.
- The answers also appear on **each attendee's profile**.

### 8.6 Add additional event admins

Two steps:

#### Step 1: invite colleagues

1. **Path:** `Admin → Invitations`
2. Click **[Add new invitation]**, enter name and email, **[OK]**.
3. When the invitation appears in the list, open it again, click **[Send this invitation]** at the bottom left, then **[OK]**.

#### Step 2: grant admin permissions

1. **Path:** `Admin → General → Permissions`
2. Enter your colleagues' email addresses, choose a suggestion or confirm with **[ENTER]**.
3. Click **[Save]**.

> **Caution:** If the email address is unknown to talque, you have to confirm with **[ENTER]** — only then can you click **[Save]**.

### 11.4 Test Jitsi video calls

For **1:1 calls**, data is transferred directly between devices. For **group calls**, data is routed through the **talque Jitsi server**. Both scenarios should be tested separately.

#### Test 1:1 Jitsi calls

1:1 calls are used for video conferences with individual attendees or partner company staff.

1. **Start a chat exchange:** trade a chat message with another person. Both must send and receive.
2. **Expect the call:** make sure the other person is expecting the call.
3. In the chat window, click **[+]**.
4. Choose **Start video call now**.
5. You should see your own webcam first, then both webcams once accepted.

#### Test group Jitsi calls

Group calls are used for open-space discussions and roundtables.

1. Connect with at least two other people on talque.
2. **Create a group chat:**
   - Click the **contacts button** in the top-right menu bar.
   - Choose **Groups**.
   - Click **[+]** to create a new group.
   - Add members (from contacts or by email).
   - Name the group.
3. Make sure the other people are expecting the call.
4. In the chat window, click **[+]** → **Start video call now**.
5. You should see your own webcam first, then all webcams once accepted.

### 11.5 Firewall configuration & troubleshooting

If Jitsi video calls don't work, contact your IT department and ask for the following:

#### Allow domains

- **Whitelist:** `*.talque.com`
- **Alternatively whitelist by ASN:** `AS16509`

#### Allow ports

| Port | Protocol | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **80** | TCP | HTTP traffic |
| **443** | TCP | HTTPS traffic (web app) |
| **10000–20000** | TCP & UDP | Video calls and livestreams (Jitsi media) |

> A detailed guide for your IT department is at:
